Introduction

Based on a luxury brand awareness survey which covers both luxury consumers and non-luxury consumers, up to 96% people wrote down the brand name LV when they think about luxury goods, which means LVMH, the parent company of LV, successfully spread the brand awareness among all kinds of audience. Besides the brand itself, nearly 73% of people surveyed agreed that online publications such as articles and news have driven more attention to LVMH. For instance, the acquisition of Tiffany in January of this year.

LVMH has always engaged frequently in the capital market. Its actions drive numerous public attention and also touch off the mergers and acquisition of high-end fashion brands in China. Shandong Ruyi Group has started imitating this kind of model since 2010, served as the parent company of the British classic brand Aquascutum, and major shareholder of SMCP, a French light luxury brand management group which owns Sandro, Maje, and Claudie Pierlot, also for Renown, a Japanese menswear group. 

However, at the start of 2019, instead of achievements what finally came across to the company that used to be dubbed as "LVMH of China" by Bloomberg are billions of unredeemed debt and risks could not be predicted. One of the biggest deals operated by Ruyi --- purchasing 70% shares of the Swiss luxury brand Bally at $720 million also ended in failure in December 2020 due to the struggle in financial liquidity.1

LVMH

As a successful model of mergers and acquisitions in the fashion industry, one year later after buying 80% of German luggage group Rimowa, LVMH became the world’s biggest luxury group and overtook the sixth-largest global oil company Total in May 3, 2017, with 117 billion euros market capitalization. It stands in the first place of French market capitalization. Bernard Arnault, the 71-year-old chairman of LVMH, was ranked by Forbes as the third richest Billionaire with 170.7B USD net worth.2 Under his leadership, the company’s net profit grew by 76 times between 1990 ~ 2019.3
