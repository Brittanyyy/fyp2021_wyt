5. Conclusion

This paper aimed to explore reasons why the luxury group Louis Vuitton Moët Hennessy could keep the leading position in recent years. Six dimensions were included which were based on the annual report of each luxury group and stretched from 2008 to 2020.

With a total market value of around 45 billion euros, the world's largest luxury group LVMH has 75 brands, covering five business sectors including Fashion and Leather Goods, Watches and Jewelry, Perfumes and Cosmetics, Wine and Spirits, Selective Retailing and other activities. Each section takes popular brands as essence and promotes integral development through them. By the end of 2020, 5003 stores all around the world contain more than 470 stores of Louis Vitton that are directly operated by the store manager which in order to customize services and maximize the profit.

The company's growth mainly comes from improving the profitability of existing brands, strengthening synergy effects, mergers and acquisitions of mature brands, and cultivations of growing brands. Its extensions mainly occurred in 1990, the peak period of mergers and acquisitions appeared in the luxury industry, and the late time of the Asian and world financial crisis, which brought with it three peaks in revenue. Business segments expand from soft luxury to hard luxury and focus on mergers and acquisitions of Fashion & Leather Goods throughout the whole development.

In Fashion & Leather Goods, except for the core brand Louis Vuitton, all brands are merged to set up the brand matrix in distinct levels. The group became the third-largest Watch & Jewelry group through this strategy, also same with Selective Retailing which pursues the most profitable section --- Fashion and Leather Good. For Perfume & Cosmetics, the company acquired 6 major cosmetics companies in 2011 and collaboratively developed all of them together. Compared with other business sectors, Wine and Spirits is more mediocre without any frequent operations and attractive public attention but carried out excellent results in the margin.

In 2020, the Fashion & Leather Goods accounted for 47% of revenue, followed by Selective Retailing and other, which is 23%, Perfumes & Cosmetics takes 13%, Wine & Spirits is 11%, and Watches & Jewelry occupied 9%. Fashion & Leather Goods and Wine & Spirits ‘s average operating profit margin in the past 5 years was 31.6% and 30.5%, while other businesses are around 10%.

Overall, in mergers and acquisitions, besides focusing heavily on the product’s history and culture to diversify and enrich the brand matrix, LVMH also changes the fashion trends with powerful artists such as operations in Louis Vuitton which activates and reshapes the image of a brand with trouble situations. Additionally, the timing of purchasing is one of the main points of mergers and acquisitions which mostly concentrated on the economic downturn, brand management difficulties, and family revolution. Highly decentralized and geographically wide business scopes also increase the company’s ability to cope with economic cycles and fashion changes which successfully ensure the leading position as a hedge fund from various perspectives.
